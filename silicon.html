Silicon Valley Timeline
1938
The beginning of Silicon Valley.
 

David Packard and William Hewlett. In 1938, these two founded the computer giant Hewlett-Packard in a small garage in Palo Alto.

1975
The Altair 8800 is released on the market.
 
The Altair 8800, developed by Ed Roberts and his company MITS in 1975, was sold as a DIY kit for $397 through the American magazine Popular Electronics.
1976
Steve Wozniak and Steve Jobs presented the Apple I at the HCC.
 
The Apple I was an early personal computer and the first to combine a keyboard with a microprocessor and a connection to a monitor.
1977
Apple II Presented
 
The Apple II (stylized as apple ][) is an 8-bit home computer and one of the world's first highly successful mass-produced microcomputer products.
1980
Daniel Kotte Employee Apple
 
Daniel Kottke is an American businessman known for being a college friend of Steve Jobs and one of the first employees of Apple Inc.
1984
Launch of Macintosh
 
The Apple Macintosh—later rebranded as the Macintosh 128K—is the original Apple Macintosh personal computer. It played a pivotal role in establishing desktop publishing as a general office function.
1991
Wi-Fi is introduced
 
In 1991 in the Netherlands, the NCR Corporation and AT&T invented the precursor to 802.11, intended for use in cashier systems, under the name WaveLAN.
1992
Windows 3.1
 
Windows 3.1x was an updated version of Windows 3.0, which was launched in 1992. Similar to Windows 1.0 and Windows 2.0, Windows 3.1 was based on MS-DOS. Windows 3.1 was the fourth version of Microsoft Windows.
1995
Windows 95
 
Windows 95 is the first 32-bit consumer version of Microsoft's operating system Windows. It was released on August 24, 1995, accompanied by an advertising campaign that led customers to wait in lines overnight at stores where Windows 95 was sold.
1998
Google introduced
 
Google LLC is an American multinational technology company that specializes in various fields including artificial intelligence, online advertising, search engine technology, cloud computing, computer software, quantum computing, e-commerce, and consumer electronics.
2004
Facebook Introduced
 
Facebook is an online social media and social networking service founded in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg and his fellow Harvard College students and roommates Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes. It is now owned by the American technology giant Meta Platforms.
2005
Youtube introduced
 
YouTube is an online video sharing and social media platform with its headquarters in San Bruno, California, United States. It was launched on February 14, 2005, by Steve Chen, Chad Hurley, and Jawed Karim and is accessible worldwide.
2006
Twitter was founded.
 
Twitter was created in March 2006 by Jack Dorsey, Noah Glass, Biz Stone, and Evan Williams.
2007
The first iPhone was presented.
 
On June 29, 2007, the first-generation iPhone became available for sale in the United States. Two versions of the first-generation iPhone were sold at launch, one with 4 gigabytes of storage and another with 8 gigabytes of storage.
